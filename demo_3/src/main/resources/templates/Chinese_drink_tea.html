Chinese tea history

The origin of the "Chinese tea history" has so far been divided, and the controversy has not yet been decided. Generally speaking, there are pre-Qin, Western Han, and the Three Kingdoms. Tea appeared in a cultural appearance. It was in the Jin and Northern Dynasties. The earliest favorite tea was the literati. After the Kaiyuan in the Tang Dynasty, China’s "tea ceremony" was a big one, and the wind of drinking tea was filled with dynasty, and the wind of tea drinking in the Song Dynasty was increasingly popular. Tea god Lu Yu has a book called "Tea Classics". The Shennong Bencao Classic also records knowledge about tea.

As early as before the Qin and Han Dynasties, tea in China was prevalent in Sichuan. In the Western Han Dynasty, tea was a special product of Sichuan. It was passed to the capital Chang'an in the tribute. It turned out that the ancient Sichuan East Hubei was the birthplace of tea trees, and this is the earliest place of the Three Emperors and Five Emperors. Tea culture In a broad sense, the natural science of tea and the humanities of tea refer to the sum of the material and spiritual wealth associated with tea created in the historical practice of human society. In a narrow sense, the humanities that focus on tea mainly refer to the function of tea for the spirit and society. Since the natural science of tea has formed an independent system, the tea culture that is often spoken now focuses on the humanities.
