Macaroni

Macaroni is a famous Italian dish.In the 18th century, makaroni, a noodle and noodle shop owner near Naples, Italy, had his little daughter roll noodles into hollow strips and hang them on a clothesline while playing. Macaroni boiled the strips and mixed them with tomato sauce. He built the world's first macaroni factory and named it after himself.

Macaroni is a famous pasta in western countries, which can be solid or hollow. Macaroni, also known as macaroni internationally, is a flour product made from wheat flour. Macaroni sauce can be left in hollow macaroni and is less likely to dry. The method of making macaroni is also very exquisite: boil water, put the macaroni into the pot, repeatedly add cold water several times, until the macaroni cook through, remove the filtered water, put the right amount of salt mix well. Bring to a boil, add peas, drain shortly after opening and cover with macaroni. Prepare small pieces of beef and saute in oil for a few seconds before serving. Add a little oil, macaroni, water, salt, a little sugar, butter (to taste) and tomato sauce. It's important to add water, which should be about two-thirds of the macaroni. The next step is to wait for the juice to gradually enter the macaroni hole, which needs 20 to 30 minutes of continuous stirring. Stir in beef, MSG, and cook for a few minutes. Stir in half a pound of minced meat with wine, soy sauce, and salt. Add chopped spring Onions and ginger. Heat the pot, put some oil, stir fry the meat stuffing, color change cooked, into the tomato sauce, stir fry, burn until the color of the tomato sauce is dark red, sauce slightly thick can start the pot, sprinkle on the macaroni, mix well. Or put the mince sauce into a bowl and eat as much as you like. After eating, it will leave you endless aftertaste.
